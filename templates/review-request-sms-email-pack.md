# 30-Day Review Recovery + Request Pack

## Day 0: public response

Post a calm, short reply. Do not argue facts in public. Invite direct contact.

## Day 1: private recovery message

Subject: Following up on your recent experience with {{business_name}}

Hi {{customer_name}}, this is {{owner_name}} from {{business_name}}. I saw your review and I’m sorry the experience did not meet expectations. I’d like to understand what happened and see what we can reasonably do to make it better. If you’re open to it, please reply here or call/text {{contact_method}}.

## Day 3: internal fix note

- What failed?
- Could we have communicated sooner?
- Did the customer know the scope, price, and timing?
- What one checklist/process item prevents the next repeat?

## Day 7: happy-customer review request SMS

Hi {{customer_name}}, thanks again for choosing {{business_name}}. If everything went well, would you be willing to leave a quick review? It helps local customers know what to expect: {{review_link}}. Thank you — {{owner_name}}

## Day 14: email review request

Subject: Quick favor after your {{service_name}}

Hi {{customer_name}}, thanks again for working with us. If you’d recommend {{business_name}}, a short Google review would mean a lot: {{review_link}}.

If anything was off, please reply directly first so we can address it.

## Day 21: QR card copy

Happy with the work? Scan here to leave a quick review. If something was not right, contact {{owner_name}} at {{contact_method}} so we can help.

## Day 30: monthly owner review checklist

- Reply to every new review, positive or negative.
- Screenshot recurring complaints and add one process fix.
- Ask 5–10 satisfied recent customers for a review.
- Do not ask only happy customers if platform policy prohibits gating; use neutral wording.
